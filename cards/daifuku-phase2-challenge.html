Industry Challenge — Phase 2

Building on the Phase 1 monitoring infrastructure, Daifuku Manufacturing Canada Corporation identified the need to extend its digital capabilities from equipment monitoring to active production management and reporting. Specific challenges included:

  • No centralized, real-time visibility into where individual work orders stand across the plant's 20+ production cells at any given time
  • Managers lacked reporting tools to identify production bottlenecks and understand daily job flow in and out of each station
  • High volumes of jobs sharing common due dates created scheduling pressure with no system to anticipate or track it
  • No live management reporting page within the existing SMS platform — metrics and analytics had to be developed and validated before they could be surfaced to managers
  • Collaborative Robot (Cobot) programming for different part geometries required repetitive custom scripting, slowing deployment of new jobs
  • No automated mechanism for scanning and logging work orders prior to Cobot job execution
  • No accessible work-order document management system to reference for current jobs
