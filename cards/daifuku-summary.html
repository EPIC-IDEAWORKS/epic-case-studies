Public Summary

Daifuku Manufacturing Canada Corporation is a leader in custom material handling systems, producing solutions for the automotive, airport, and industrial sectors from its Hamilton, Ontario facility. The facility operates more than 20 simultaneous production cells, with equipment ranging from modern robotics to machinery dating back to the 1950s. Daifuku faced compounding challenges: manual production tracking, untracked downtime, excess material waste, high energy consumption, and no real-time visibility into plant operations.

Beginning in 2023, Daifuku and Mohawk College's Energy & Power Innovation Centre (EPIC) embarked on a two-phase digital transformation collaboration spanning over two years. Phase 1 delivered a plant-wide IoT monitoring system, cloud analytics platform, and a full Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions assessment — resulting in a 20% improvement in operational efficiency, a 10 to 15% reduction in material waste, and a reduction in client onboarding time from three weeks to less than one day. Phase 2 extended those foundations with live production reporting, a metrics and trending analytics pipeline, Cobot integration, and a work-order document management system.

Together, the two phases have positioned Daifuku as a data-driven manufacturer with a scalable digital infrastructure it fully owns, spanning plant-wide IoT monitoring, energy management, carbon emissions accounting, live production reporting, and on-device Cobot automation.
