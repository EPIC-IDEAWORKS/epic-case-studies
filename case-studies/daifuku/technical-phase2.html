Industry Challenge — Phase 2

Building on the Phase 1 monitoring infrastructure, Daifuku Manufacturing Canada Corporation identified the need to extend its digital capabilities from equipment monitoring to active production management and reporting. Specific challenges included:

  • No centralized, real-time visibility into where individual work orders stand across the plant's 20+ production cells at any given time
  • Managers lacked reporting tools to identify production bottlenecks and understand daily job flow in and out of each station
  • High volumes of jobs sharing common due dates created scheduling pressure with no system to anticipate or track it
  • No live management reporting page within the existing SMS platform — metrics and analytics had to be developed and validated before they could be surfaced to managers
  • Collaborative Robot (Cobot) programming for different part geometries required repetitive custom scripting, slowing deployment of new jobs
  • No automated mechanism for scanning and logging work orders prior to Cobot job execution
  • No accessible work-order document management system to reference for current jobs

Solution & Implementation — Phase 2

EPIC designed and implemented the following components as part of the Phase 2 collaboration, all building on the AWS cloud platform and SMS established in Phase 1.

1. Metrics and Trending Analytics Pipeline (Python / Jupyter Notebooks)

Before adding any reporting to the SMS platform, EPIC built a Python-based Jupyter Notebook analytics pipeline to acquire, store, and iteratively develop production metrics in close collaboration with Daifuku's management team. Metrics developed and validated included:

  • High volumes of jobs sharing a common due date, to surface scheduling pressure before it becomes a production crisis
  • Job counts by station with threshold monitoring, to flag overloaded cells in real time
  • Daily job flow in and out of stations, to track throughput and identify stoppages
  • Exploratory additional metrics to surface previously untracked problem areas across the plant

2. Live Management Reporting Page in the SMS Platform

Once the right metrics were established through the pipeline, EPIC implemented them as a live reporting page within the existing Phase 1 SMS platform. The reporting page includes:

  • Pie chart showing distribution of jobs by plant
  • Average completions per day, capacity gap, and days-to-clear metrics
  • Modified Z-Score anomaly detection for duration-of-stay at each cell (based on median), to surface outlier cells indicating production problems
  • Net jobs and median stay-at-cell reporting
  • Jobs completed per cell with selectable date ranges

3. Cobot Integration — Generic Shape Programming Script

EPIC updated and upgraded the generic scripting framework for Cobot shape operations, consolidating multiple custom scripts into a single flexible program designed to meet the majority of generic shapes requirements.

4. Python-based Web App — Work-Orders Document Manager

EPIC developed and implemented a semi-automated, web-based document management system for uploading and managing files and schematics tied to existing work orders, accessible from any device on the on-premises network.

5. Barcode Scanner Integration

EPIC integrated a barcode scanner with the Cobot to enable automatic scanning and logging of work orders prior to job execution. This integration was implemented on-device, with research and initial planning completed for future API-based connection to the cloud platform.

6. Workforce Training and Knowledge Transfer

EPIC designed and delivered training programs to ensure Daifuku's workforce can operate, maintain, and build upon the SMS reporting page and Cobot integrations without ongoing dependency on EPIC.

Impact — Phase 2

Industry Partner

  • Managers gain real-time production reporting through a live page in the existing SMS platform, covering work order status, job flow, cell performance, and scheduling pressure
  • The metrics and trending pipeline surfaces bottlenecks and capacity gaps that were previously invisible to operations leadership
  • Dashboard anomaly detection (Z-Score methodology) provides an early-warning system for cells experiencing abnormal dwell times

Sectors: Who This Model Applies To

The combined Phase 1 and Phase 2 framework — covering IoT monitoring, cloud-hosted shop management software, real-time production dashboards, carbon emissions accounting, work order tracking, and Cobot integration — is applicable to organizations in:

  • Manufacturing (automotive, aerospace, electronics, consumer goods): any facility with multi-cell or multi-line production and legacy equipment
  • Logistics and warehousing: facilities using automated or semi-automated material handling equipment
  • Food and beverage manufacturing: production scheduling and work order tracking across processing lines
  • Industrial maintenance operations: predictive and condition-based maintenance enabled by real-time data pipelines
  • Any SME seeking Industry 4.0 digital transformation with clear bottleneck-identification, energy management, or production tracking use cases
