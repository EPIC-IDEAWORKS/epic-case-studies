EPIC Technical Case Study — Phase 1

Digital Transformation at Daifuku Manufacturing Canada: Phase 1 — IoT Monitoring, Energy Management & Cloud Analytics

A collaboration between Mohawk College's Energy & Power Innovation Centre (EPIC) and Daifuku Manufacturing Canada Corporation (formerly Jervis Webb).

Phase 1 September 2023 – August 2024 Hamilton, Ontario, Canada
OCI C2C NRC-IRAP
Phase 1 Summary

Daifuku Manufacturing Canada Corporation is a leader in custom material handling systems, producing solutions for the automotive, airport, and industrial sectors from its Hamilton, Ontario facility. The facility operates more than 20 simultaneous production cells, with equipment ranging from modern robotics to machinery dating back to the 1950s. Daifuku faced compounding challenges: manual production tracking, untracked downtime, excess material waste, high energy consumption, and no real-time visibility into plant operations. Daifuku's corporate office also mandated that all plants track, quantify, and reduce Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions — and the Hamilton facility lacked the expertise and tools to begin.

Beginning in September 2023, Daifuku and Mohawk College's Energy & Power Innovation Centre (EPIC) launched Phase 1 of a multi-phase digital transformation. EPIC deployed a plant-wide IoT monitoring system connecting legacy and modern equipment to a unified cloud analytics platform, installed energy and natural gas submetering at the work-cell level, implemented RFID-based work-order tracking, and concurrently conducted a full Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions assessment using the GHG Protocol framework. Phase 1 resulted in a 20% improvement in operational efficiency, a 10 to 15% reduction in material waste, and a reduction in client onboarding time from three weeks to less than one day. Daifuku also gained a verified carbon footprint baseline and is now commercializing the platform to other manufacturers.

Industry Challenge — Phase 1

In 2023, Daifuku Manufacturing Canada Corporation approached EPIC to address the high energy consumption of its production processes. As the collaboration developed, the scope expanded to encompass the following core operational challenges:

  • Manual production management: Daifuku relied on manual processes to assign tasks, monitor progress, and track energy and equipment usage across more than 20 simultaneous production cells.
  • Aging and heterogeneous equipment: Equipment ranged from modern systems to machinery dating back to the 1950s, resulting in inconsistent monitoring capabilities and limited visibility into usage, energy consumption, and production flow.
  • Untracked inefficiencies: Manual processes resulted in production inefficiencies, excess material waste, and untracked downtime with no mechanism to quantify or address these losses.
  • Energy consumption: No visibility into plant-wide or work-cell-level energy use, preventing targeted interventions or strategic equipment shutdowns for effective peak shaving to manage Global Adjustment charges.
  • Carbon emissions: Daifuku's corporate office mandated that all plants track, quantify, and reduce Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions. The Hamilton facility lacked the expertise and tools to quantify its full carbon footprint or identify reduction opportunities.
  • Client onboarding: New client production setup required approximately three weeks due to manual configuration and tracking processes.

Solution & Implementation — Phase 1

EPIC collaborated with the Daifuku team to design and deploy a custom IoT monitoring system integrated with a cloud-based analytics platform plant-wide. The solution was built to accommodate Daifuku's heterogeneous equipment inventory and flexible, multi-client production environment.

IoT Monitoring System

EPIC deployed IoT sensors and data acquisition hardware across the Hamilton facility, connecting legacy and modern equipment to a unified cloud platform. The system provided:

  • Plant-wide electricity and natural gas monitoring and energy submetering at the work-cell level, enabling identification of high-consumption equipment and targeted equipment shutdown strategies
  • Real-time equipment status monitoring across all active production cells
  • Equipment usage and downtime tracking, quantifying previously invisible production losses
  • RFID-based part and work-order tracking through the production flow
  • Performance KPIs surfaced through the analytics platform

GHG Emissions Assessment

Concurrently with the IoT and platform work, EPIC's Low Carbon Readiness Program (LCRP) conducted a full Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions assessment of the Daifuku Hamilton facility using the GHG Protocol and Scope 3 Technical Guidance as the framework. The LCRP team:

  • Reviewed Daifuku's existing Scope 1 and 2 work, performed calculations to quantify facility emissions, and provided guidance to establish an emissions baseline
  • Identified the four most relevant Scope 3 categories and calculated associated emissions: Category 1 (Purchased Goods and Services), Category 4 (Upstream Transport and Distribution), Category 5 (Waste from Operations), and Category 11 (Use of Sold Products)
  • Provided guidance to improve Daifuku's data collection and tracking processes for all four Scope 3 categories
  • Identified and recommended high-level carbon reduction opportunities across Scope 1, 2, and each Scope 3 category

Cloud Analytics Platform

EPIC designed and deployed a scalable, multi-tenant cloud platform using AWS IoT Core, DynamoDB, Lambda, and Amplify. Next.js and TypeScript were selected to ensure long-term maintainability and to simplify future updates, feature expansion, and scalability. The platform serves as the central hub for all monitoring, analytics, and reporting.

Impact — Phase 1

Industry Partner

Phase 1 gave Daifuku Manufacturing Canada Corporation its first real-time, plant-wide view of production, equipment, and energy use. Specific impacts include:

  • Digitization of legacy infrastructure that was previously unmonitorable
  • Foundation for predictive maintenance and data-driven capital planning
  • A new commercial opportunity: Daifuku is now offering the platform to other manufacturers, creating a revenue stream that did not exist before the collaboration
  • Reduction in client onboarding from three weeks to less than one day, directly improving Daifuku's service capacity and competitiveness
  • A complete, quantified carbon footprint for the Hamilton facility covering Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions, putting the plant in good standing with Daifuku's corporate sustainability requirements
  • Functional GHG tracking tools and reduction recommendations that Daifuku can use independently on an ongoing basis

EPIC

Through Phase 1, EPIC's team advanced expertise in:

  • Plant-wide IoT deployment in complex, legacy-rich manufacturing environments
  • Multi-tenant cloud platform architecture for industrial monitoring applications
  • Energy submetering integration and analytics for manufacturing facilities
  • RFID-based work-order and production tracking systems
  • GHG Protocol application for Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions quantification in a manufacturing context
  • Scope 3 category identification, data collection guidance, and carbon reduction recommendations for a material handling manufacturer
Sectors: Who This Model Applies To

The Phase 1 framework — IoT monitoring, cloud-based analytics, energy and natural gas submetering, RFID work-order tracking, KPI dashboards, and Scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions assessment — is applicable to organizations in:

  • Manufacturing (automotive, aerospace, electronics, consumer goods): any facility with multi-cell or multi-line production, legacy equipment, or a mandate to quantify and reduce carbon emissions
  • Industrial and process facilities: operations requiring plant-wide energy monitoring, equipment usage tracking, and targeted peak-shaving strategies
  • Logistics and warehousing: facilities with material flow, work-order tracking, or regulatory emissions reporting requirements
  • Food and beverage manufacturing: operations seeking submetering and energy management combined with Scope 3 emissions accounting across the supply chain
  • Any SME with a corporate GHG reporting mandate, high energy costs, or production inefficiencies that are currently untracked and unquantified
